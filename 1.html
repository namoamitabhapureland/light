Q: Why design this digital dojo?
The Amitabha Sutra states: "One cannot be born in that land with a lack of good roots, blessings, and causal connections."

The Infinite Life Sutra also states: "Regarding the lower tier... if they awaken the unsurpassable Bodhi mind, single-mindedly focus their thoughts, and recite the name of the Buddha of Infinite Life even ten times, desiring to be born in that land... upon the moment of death, they will see this Buddha in a dream and will be born there."

Therefore, diligently accumulating merit in this world is by no means falling into the delusion of "clinging to self-power," but rather building upon the absolute bedrock of "100% profound faith in Amitabha's vow of reception."

The moment a practitioner awakens the unsurpassable Bodhi mind and sincerely vows to be born in the Pure Land, a dedicated lotus flower is instantly manifested in Amitabha's seven-jewel pond. Persevering in accumulating pure karma merit is precisely using an ordinary being's faith and vow to correspond with the Buddha's fruit of virtue; deeply believing in the Buddha-wisdom, inconceivable wisdom, ineffable wisdom, boundless Mahayana wisdom, and the unequaled, peerless, supreme wisdom, cultivating all merits without a single thought of doubt.
Q: Affiliation and Stance?
This system is a purely independent Dharma tool, maintaining a neutral and objective stance:

1. Independent: Not affiliated with any secular institution, religious organization, group, or individual.
2. Non-profit: No memberships, no ads, and strictly no acceptance of sponsorships, fundraising, or donations.
3. Open Tool: Created solely as an auxiliary tool for practice, providing a stress-free space for daily devotion.
Q: Sahā vs. Pure Land Time?
The Avatamsaka Sutra states: "One eon in the Sahā World equals one day and night in the Pure Land."
This reflects their macro physical time contrast.

Conversely, the Infinite Life Sutra states: "Cultivating good in the Sahā World for one day and night surpasses doing so in the Pure Land for a hundred years."
This is the merit conversion rate.

【 Deep Meaning 】 Despite its severe hardships, the Sahā World is the fastest place to accumulate merit. Every pure thought and recitation here requires immense vow-power, generating true, unconditioned merit.

Thus, practitioners are encouraged to seize every moment here, advancing diligently through adversity to gather supreme provisions for Awakening.
Q: Meaning of Ten Recitations?
Centered on the "Ten Recitations Method." Every completion embodies Faith, Vows, and Practice. The rise in merit is not blind counting, but a true measure of mental transformation and the cleansing of defilements.
Q: The Subtle Mystery Behind Spiritual Stages and the Transformation of Sacred Images?
The core spirit of Mahayana Buddhism lies in awakening Bodhicitta, practicing the Bodhisattva path, and ultimately attaining Equal Enlightenment. Synthesizing the teachings of the Avatamsaka Sutra and the Sutra of the Jeweled Necklace of the Bodhisattva's Primary Practices, a practitioner must diligently advance through fifty-two Bodhisattva stages from the initial aspiration to the perfect realization of the Supreme Buddha Path.

From the First to the Second Stage of Faith, and further to the perfection of the Ten Minds of the Faith Stage—namely, the minds of Faith, Mindfulness, Diligence, Wisdom, Concentration, Non-retrogression, Dedication, Protection, Precepts, and Vow—it can take up to ten thousand great kalpas of alternating progress and retrogression to truly awaken the Great Bodhicitta, regardless of whether one's spiritual roots are sharp or dull. Advancing further toward the First Bhumi and up to the Eighth Bhumi requires countless asankhyeya kalpas.

The system harmoniously integrates the doctrines of the Infinite Life Sutra, the Avatamsaka Sutra, and the Lotus Sutra, mapping the daily provisions of merit and wisdom onto the progression of the Faith, Vow, Practice, Goodness, and Virtue stages through the skillful means of "using the illusory to cultivate the true." By visually previewing the pristine Dharma-nature of the Pure Land of Ultimate Bliss, it actualizes the practice where "all cultivate the virtues of Samantabhadra." This symbolizes the practitioner's elevation from a state where "the mind turns with the environment" to a realization where "the environment turns with the mind," allowing a direct perception of the intrinsic purity and solemnity of one's true nature.
Q: How does the system interact with me?
The Dharma treasure has a spirit; it responds to sincerity. The visual transformations respond profoundly to the practitioner's persistent vows. Like drinking water, one knows for oneself whether it is cold or warm; this realm of realization is left for you to experience.

Q: Why a Web App and not a traditional App?
To maintain the dojo's "absolute purity" and "privacy."

Traditional apps require permissions or external servers. This tool uses pure frontend technology with no server tracking and no database storage. All your data lives "100%" on your personal device, with zero risk of leaks.
